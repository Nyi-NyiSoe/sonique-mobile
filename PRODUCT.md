# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sonique is for people who want a simple personal music app experience: browsing tracks, albums, artists, liked songs, and playlists without the complexity of a full commercial streaming product. It also supports lightweight artist-side workflows for uploading tracks and albums, mainly for a hobby project built alongside a backend service.

## Product Purpose

Sonique is a small Spotify-style Flutter music client. It exists to make the core music flow work clearly: sign in, browse music, play songs, save favorites, manage playlists, and let artist users upload releases. Success means the app feels straightforward, responsive, and coherent across the main listener and artist paths, even with a smaller feature set.

## Brand Personality

Simple, works, personal. The interface should feel familiar enough for a music app user to understand immediately, with enough restraint that it does not feel like a loud Spotify copy. The tone should be direct and practical, using clear labels and short messages.

## Anti-references

Avoid an over-saturated green palette or a screen that feels like a full Spotify clone with fewer features. Avoid heavy animation, elaborate page transitions, decorative motion, and visual effects that could reduce performance on mobile devices. Avoid adding complex premium-streaming patterns that the product does not actually support.

## Design Principles

- Keep the core music task visible: playback, browsing, saving, and playlist actions should be easy to reach.
- Preserve familiarity without copying: use standard music-app patterns, but keep Sonique quieter and more personal.
- Prefer restrained color: use green as an action and selection accent, not as a dominant decoration on every surface.
- Make performance part of the design: use light state transitions only where they clarify feedback.
- Design for the actual scope: small feature set, clear flows, no fake complexity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Keep motion minimal and provide reduced-motion behavior for any future animations. Do not rely on color alone for state, especially for liked, selected, error, warning, and success feedback.
